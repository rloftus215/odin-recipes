Easy vegetarian curry recipe

A vegetarian curry is a delightful and aromatic dish, bursting with rich, complex flavors from a blend of spices like cumin, coriander, and turmeric. The creamy sauce, enhanced with coconut milk, creates a comforting and satisfying meal. Perfectly balanced with heat and depth, this curry pairs wonderfully with rice or naan, offering a hearty and nourishing experience.

Ingredients

  • 1 large potato, diced
  • 1 small butternut squash, peeled, deseeded and diced
  • 1 aubergine, diced
  • 6 tbsp tikka masala paste
  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 680g-700g jar tomato passata
  • 400g can coconut milk
  • 2 red peppers, sliced
  • 2 courgettes, diced
  • few coriander sprigs, to serve
  • rice or naan bread, to serve

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 200C/180C fan/gas 6. Toss the potato, squash and aubergine with 2 tbsp curry paste and 2 tbsp oil in a large roasting tin. Season, then roast for 30 mins.
  2. Meanwhile, make the sauce. Fry the onions in the remaining oil in a large pan until softened and golden â€“ add a splash of water if they start to dry out. Stir in the remaining curry paste, cook for 3 mins, then add the passata, coconut milk and 100ml water. Simmer for a few mins.
  3. When the vegetables are roasted, tip them into the sauce with the peppers and courgettes. Simmer for 10-15 mins until tender. Scatter with coriander and serve.
